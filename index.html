Metroid Unity

This is a remake of some of the initial areas of the 1986 classic game, "Metroid", made using the Unity game engine and C#. This project was made over the course of three weeks working with a team of two. My work was focused on player movement and animations, along with the game's environment.

Radiant SaiDaiOuFukkatsu

This game is a single-level bullet hell shoot 'em up created as a solo project over the course of two weeks. It features a unique enemy capture system which lets the player capture enemies as drones to increase their own firepower. This was definitely a passion project of mine since I love this genre, so I hope you enjoy playing my take on it!

ROOTS: 2077

This is a multiplayer cart-racing game made in 48 hours for a game jam at the University of Michigan. It features a strange cast of characters, many useable items, and an intentionally absurd storyline meant to parody JRPGs. I was responsible for most of the core gameplay functionality, including the car driving and drifting physics, along with the lap system and several of the items.

G.S.R.

This is a two-player anti-gravity racing game with a focus on combat. It was made over the course of 6-weeks for a game dev class at the University of Michigan. Players must navigate a rollercoaster-like track filled with twists and loops while using their chosen weapon to destroy drones and damage their opponent. I personally worked on the car physics, weapons, AI cars, and various other systems.
